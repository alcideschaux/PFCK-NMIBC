Title: Expression Status and Prognostic Value of PDL1, FOXP3, CD8 and Ki67 in Non-Muscle Invasive Bladder Urothelial Carcinoma

Authors’ list: Gunes Guner (1), Margaret Cocks (1), Diana Taheri (1), Mark W. Ball (2), Stephania M. Bezerra (1), Alan Meeker (1), Maria del Carmen Rodriguez (1), Alcides Chaux (1,4), Arthur Burnett (2), George Netto (1,2,3)

Authors’ affiliations: Departments of (1) Pathology, (2) Urology and (3) Oncology, The Johns Hopkins Medical Institutions, Baltimore, MD; (4) Department of Scientific Research, Norte University, Asuncion, PRY.

Material & method

Analysis by histology

The analysis of PDL1, FOXP3 and CD8/ki67 expression was carried out at the TMA level. The dataset includes 61 patients with non-muscle invasive bladder urothelial carcinoma, from whom 461 TMA spots were build from their corresponding tumors. For the histologic diagnosis, “Nontumor” includes normal urothelium and papillary hyperplasia, “CIS” includes carcinoma in situ and dysplasia, “LG” and “HG” mean low-grade and high-grade noninvasive papillary urothelial carcinoma, respectively.

Outcome Analysis

The analysis of PDL1, FOXP3 and CD6/Ki67 expression was carried out at the TMA level. The dataset includes 61 patients with non-muscle invasive bladder urothelial carcinoma, from whom 461 TMA spots were build from their corresponding tumors. The maximum value accross TMA spots was considered as representative of the marker expression and used for data analysis.

Outcome endpoints

The analysis was carried out using paired events. For any case, the paired event consisted in the pair formed by 2 consecutive diagnoses during follow up. Thus, a case could have one or more paired events. The total of paired events analyzed was 143. For each marker, The considered outcomes included:

  • Tumor recurrence at next diagnosis: Tumor reappeared showing a similar or lower-grade/stage lession.
  • Tumor recurrence at any time: Tumor had recurred at least once during follow-up.
  • Tumor grade progression at next diagnosis: Tumor reappeared showing a higher-grade lession.
  • Tumor grade progression at any time: Tumor had showed grade progression at least once during follow-up.
  • Tumor stage progression at next diagnosis: Tumor reappeared showing a higher-stage lession.
  • Tumor stage progression at any time: Tumor had showed stage progression at least once during follow-up.

Scale tranformation

Due to the marked skewness of the data distribution in numeric variables, for association and outcome analyses we chose to depict the plots using a logarithmic transformation. Expression values were transformed using the following formula:

\[ x_i = log_{10}(x + 1)\]

where \(x\) is the corresponding value of the marker expression (count, percentage or H-score) and \(x_i\) the transformed value, used for the plots. It is worth noting that this transformation does not change the value of the statistical tests. Tables show the actual values, not the transformed values.

Visual display

  • Univariate plots: Numeric variables were plotted using histograms and denstiy plots, along with a summary table of central tendency and dispersion values. Categorical variables were plotted using bar charts of absolute frequencies and a summary table of absolute and relative frequencies.
  • Bivariate plots: Numeric variables (predictors) were plotted using boxplots and density plots, along with a summary table of central tendency and dispersion values, per factor levels of the categorical (outcome) variables, and the results of the Kruskal-Wallis test. Categorical variables were plotted using bar charts of absolute and relative frequencies, along with a summary table of absolute and relative frequencies, and the results of the Pearson’s Chi-squared test.

Statistical tests

For the asssociation and outcome analysis we used the Kruskal-Wallis rank sum test and the Pearson’s Chi-squared test. Data was analyzed and plots were generated using R version 3.3.1 (2016-06-21).
